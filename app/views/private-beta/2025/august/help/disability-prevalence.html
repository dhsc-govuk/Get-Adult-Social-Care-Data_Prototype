What this measures
The proportion of people living within the selected administrative area within England who assessed their day-to-day activities as limited by long-term physical or mental health conditions or illnesses are considered disabled. This definition of a disabled person meets the harmonised standard for measuring disability and is in line with the Equality Act (2010).

Census 2021 from the Office for National Statistics (opens in new tab)

Methodology
This dataset provides Census 2021 estimates that classify usual residents in England and Wales by long-term health problems or disabilities. The estimates are as at Census Day, 21 March 2021.
Limitations
The data is based on survey responses, which may be subject to response bias.

These are disability prevalence estimates for Census Day (21 March 2021), not the present day.
Data definitions
'People with disabilities' refers to individuals who report limitations in daily activities due to long-term physical or mental illnesses.
