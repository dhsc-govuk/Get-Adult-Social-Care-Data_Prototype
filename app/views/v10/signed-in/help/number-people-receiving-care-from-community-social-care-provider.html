What this measures
The total number of people receiving care from care providers registered as community social care providers with the Care Quality Commission (CQC) as recorded in Capacity Tracker.
Source
Capacity Tracker from the Department of Health and Social Care (opens in new tab)

Methodology

This is calculated by summing the total number of people receiving care within each provider registered as a community social care provider with the CQC.

Regional and national averages are calculated by summing total figures and dividing by number of LAs relevant to the area.

Care providers registered with the CQC must update this information at least monthly using the Capacity Tracker tool. The mandated reporting period is between the 8th and 14th every month, or the next working day if the 14th falls on a weekend or holiday.

Service user counts are suppressed at provider level where they fall below 6, to protect the confidentiality of individuals. At local authority, regional, and national level, figures are also suppressed where there are too few care providers contributing to a total, to avoid identification of individual provider figures by subtraction. Suppressed values appear as 0. The Isles of Scilly are excluded at all geographic levels.

Limitations

Care providers may update their Capacity Tracker data at different times outside the reporting period.

As a result, the data does not provide a snapshot of all providers at the same time. It reflects the most recent information available when the data was retrieved.

The data is self-reported and not independently verified.
